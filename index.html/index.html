The Most Trending Watches of 2025

The Most Watch

This year of 2025, new product inspiration and volume energy were relatively subdued, resulting in a widespread lack of genuine creativity and original watches, with a lot of emphasis placed on refining or expanding existing products and success stories. “Heritage” watch models continue to be highly popular with major brands at all prices. With that said, a close examination of the past does help watches to perfect and even improve old ideas with fresh technology or manufacturing techniques. The watch industry today probably makes the highest performance sport watches and the prettiest dress watches it has ever made. These might not be the freshest ideas, but at least the luxury watch industry continues to (mostly) competently perform the tasks they are traditionally well-known for.

Rolex Land-Dweller 40 What you need to Know and why is so Special.

The Rolex Land-Dweller 40 is a new model in the Rolex lineup, known for its distinctive honeycomb dial and innovative features. It features a new movement, the Caliber 7135, and a new Dynapulse escapement, which is more energy-efficient than traditional escapements. The 40mm case is available in Oystersteel, Everose gold, and platinum, with matching bezels and bracelets.

TAG Heuer Formula 1 Solargraph What you need to Know and why is so Special.

The striking white opalin dial contrasts with the vibrant green flange, creating a dynamic visual appeal. Black lacquered hands and indexes, treated with Super-LumiNova®, ensure clear readability even in the dark. The TH-Polylight bezel underscores the Maison's commitment to innovative materials.

Tudor pelagos ultra titanium What you need to Know and why is so Special.

The apex predator of toolishly modern Swiss dive watches now has a new flagship model, as Tudor has just released the new Pelagos Ultra. Now 43mm wide and rated to a whopping 1000 meters, the Ultra also gets Master Chronometer timekeeping as the Pelagos expands with the addition of this larger and higher-spec offering.

Other watches to see


Patek Philippe Calatrava 6196P Learn More Here.

Never mind the outrageous desk clock. Never mind the smoked sapphire dial perpetual calendar. Never mind the quadruple complication. The standout piece from Patek Philippe — and one of the standouts from the whole show — was the Patek Philippe Calatrava 6196P with its salmon dial and black hands and indices. An exercise in perfection, the watch is as unexpected as it is beautiful. Black polished hands and markers on a salmon (the brand says “rose-gilt opaline”) dial? I wouldn’t expect it from most brands, let alone the stuffy pooh-bahs at Patty P. And yet here it is, understated yet bold, refined with just a dash of edge. On the wrist, it is unsurprisingly effortless and begs to be worn all the time. With this release, Patek continued to make efforts to create less staid and stale watches, certainly hoping to court a younger, contemporary consumer base. If it continues like this, it may well be successful.

Zenith G.F.J. Learn More Here.

2025 marks 160 years of Zenith, and while its trilogy of royal blue ceramic chronographs grabbed most of the headlines at Watches and Wonders, we believe the best of the brand’s commemorative models is the one that comparatively flew under the radar. Powered by a revival of the legendary Caliber 135 automatic movement (a platform that won 235 chronometry prizes between 1949 and 1962, a record that has yet to be surpassed), this exquisite dressy design blends the brand’s traditional focus on movement-making with a stellar eye for finishing. A stepped platinum case, unique “brick-pattern” guillochage, and a multi-stone dial surface divided between lapis lazuli and blue-toned mother of pearl create a complex, elegant presentation, but it’s the movement that truly steals the show. Genuine improvements to the classic Caliber 135 platform (an uprated 72-hour power reserve, a newly optimized gear train, and a hacking mechanism) coincide with brick-pattern guilloche and polished anglage for a powerplant that’s as beautiful as it is well-engineered. The revised Caliber 135’s performance speaks for itself, with a stellar accuracy rating of +2/-2 seconds per day. Although it’s limited to only 160 units to celebrate the brand’s anniversary, it’s easy to imagine this stellar movement making its way into future brand releases.
